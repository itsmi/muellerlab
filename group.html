About the Primary investigator

Jochen Mueller is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Civil and Systems Engineering at Johns Hopkins University and the director of the Mueller Lab – the Laboratory for Digital Fabrication and Programmable Matter.

Having grown up in a small family business for the manufacturing of steel parts, Jochen first pursued an apprenticeship as a Machinist and Mechanic at hGears. As a first in the family to attend university, this experience provided an ideal basis for his subsequent Bachelor's studies in Mechanical Engineering at the Albstadt-Sigmaringen University – which also allowed him to gain further industry experience with companies like Bosch, Exeron, and Henkel. It was during this same time and owing to the opportunities of working with Simon Park at the University of Calgary and Adrian Neild at Monash University, when Jochen developed his passion for academic research. He went on to get a Master's degree in the Nanomaterials Group with Ambrose Taylor and Anthony Kinloch at Imperial College London for his work on fiber-reinforced composite materials and the numerical simulation thereof. Jochen received his PhD from the Engineering Design and Computing group at ETH Zurich, where he worked with Kristina Shea on utilizing computers for the automated design and optimization of high-perfomance 3D printed parts. For his postdoctoral research, Jochen collaborated with Jennifer Lewis and Katia Bertoldi to develop new methods of manufacturing for active structures, mechanical metamaterials, and soft robotics at Harvard University. Since July 2021, Jochen is an Assistant Professor in the Whiting School of Engineering at Johns Hopkins University.
